In this course you learned the basics of the stock market, financial statement analysis & technical analysis. Our goal was to bring you up to speed on basics in a short period of time. But, this is just the beginning of your journey.

I have created a list of resources that I think you will find useful for your investing/ trading journey. You may choose the resources depending on your interest. But, I think all of the resources here are equally valuable.

1. The Intelligent Investor: Chances are you are already aware of this investing classic. This is a great read to understand the cigarette butt strategy that served Warren Buffett in his early days. It is a very profitable strategy to till this date, specially if you are a retail investor.

2. Berkshire Hathaway Letters to Shareholders: Every year Warren Buffett releases his letter to the Berkshire Shareholders. While parts of it may seem repetitive this is probably the single most direct resource where Warren Buffett shares his insights.

3. The Dhando Investor By Mohnish Pabrai: In this book Mohnish Pabrai shares his framework of value investing. It is a very entertaining yet insightful read on how to simplify the value investing process. In a world where we believe only complexity can yield results, this book may be an eye-opener. 

4. Elliott Wave Principle : Key to Market Behavior: This is a great book to understand Elliott wave principle, which is an advanced technique within technical analysis. The pattern that we covered in the last section as part of this course is just one of many patterns within Elliott wave principle. It is a must read if you would like to use technical analysis for trading or long term analysis.

5. Technical Analysis of Financial Markets - John Murphy: This is a great book which covers technical analysis concepts in quite a bit of detail. Though most of the concepts we have covered in our course. You may still like to read it for furthering your knowledge.

6. Harmonic Trading - Volume 1 & 2 Scott M. Carney - This is another advanced technical analysis technique, though you may find some similarity with Elliott Theory.

7. Reminiscence of a Stock Operator - This is a biography of Jessy Livermore a famous trader who was once asked to bailout the stock market. It was ranked #15 on Fortune's 75 The smartest book we know.

8. The Big Short By Micheal Lewis tracks the brief history of people who benefited by shorting the real estate market during the 2008 subprime crisis. It is a must read as it reinforces the value of original & contrarian thinking based on hard facts.

9. Technical Analysis Using Elliott Wave Theory: A short primer course with recent practical examples on Elliott wave Theory to help you quickly get started with Elliott wave theory.

10 Value Investing Strategies for the stock market: In this course you will learn the Benjamin Graham Strategy and Moat based value investing which will give you a complete understanding of how value investing works.
